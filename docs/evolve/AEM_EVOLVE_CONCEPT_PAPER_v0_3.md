## 1. Abstract

AEM-EVOLVE(TM) is a proposed extension of AEM V1.1 that governs material changes in AI systems through declared Evolution Events, evidence-bound Evolution Gates, claim-level controls, and signed Evolution Receipts.

It extends static artifact verification toward verifiable governance of transitions between system states. A controlled demonstration scenario illustrates how the system can produce a SCOPE_LIMITED Evolution Receipt while enforcing strict boundaries against clinical, diagnostic, or regulatory overclaiming.

**Core idea**: Evolvable AI requires evolvable governance.

---

## 2. Executive Summary

Agentic AI systems evolve continuously, but most governance frameworks remain static. This creates unaudited drift and overclaiming.

**Central Thesis**: Evolvable AI requires evolvable governance. Every material change must be declared, evidenced, verified, claim-limited, and fail-closed when required evidence is missing.

**Proposal**: AEM-EVOLVE(TM) converts each material change into an Evolution Event evaluated by an Evolution Gate, producing signed, machine-checkable Evolution Receipts. It builds directly on the publicly verified foundation of AEM V1.1.

A controlled technical demonstration illustrates the proposed pipeline in a constrained environment.

---

## 3. Relationship to AEM V1.1

AEM V1.1 provides public-verifiable artifact assurance: registry records, canonical hashes, signed receipts, supply-chain verification, reproducibility extensions, and Ethereum Mainnet anchors.

AEM-EVOLVE(TM) proposes to extend that foundation from static artifact assurance to governed change assurance. Instead of only asking whether an artifact is verifiable, it asks whether a material change was properly declared, evidenced, verified, claim-limited, and recorded.

---

## 4. Core Concepts

- **Evolution Event**: Structured, declared record of a material change.
- **Evolution Gate**: Decision engine that evaluates evidence sufficiency, sector rules, materiality, and claim boundaries.
- **Evolution Receipt**: Cryptographically signed artifact documenting the gate outcome.
- **Canonical Outcomes**: PASS, SCOPE_LIMITED, FAIL_CLOSED, REJECTED_OVERCLAIM, PENDING_EXTERNAL_REVIEW.
- **Anti-Overclaim Mechanism**: Mechanical control that preserves permitted claim levels even when technical performance improves.

**Core Principle**:  
**AEM-EVOLVE(TM) governs not only what an AI system is, but how it is allowed to change.**
